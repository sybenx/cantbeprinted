Why Inflation Hurts (And How Bitcoin Helps)

Rising prices mean your money buys less. Bitcoin’s fixed supply is built to fight back.

What Is Inflation?

Inflation is when prices for groceries, gas, and rent rise over time. Your $100 in 2020 buys only ~$82 worth of goods today (CPI data). Low inflation (1-3%) encourages spending, keeping the economy healthy. But high spikes, like 9% in 2022, hurt savers. Governments printing money—38% more dollars from 2020-2025—can fuel this, eroding your savings’ value.

Bitcoin’s Answer

Bitcoin’s supply is capped at 21 million coins. No one can print more, unlike dollars. This scarcity can protect your wealth: from 2015-2025, Bitcoin grew ~4,000%, outpacing inflation’s ~30%. But it’s not perfect—prices fell 20% in Q1 2025. Bitcoin’s a long-term tool, not a quick fix, and one of many options like stocks or gold.

What Can You Do?

Inflation’s a fact of life, but you can fight back. Bitcoin’s one way to protect your wealth, thanks to its fixed supply. Start small: learn how it works or buy a little to test the waters. Explore wallets and exchanges to get going safely.
