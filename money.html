A Steady Anchor in Unsteady Times

Inflation shrinks your money’s value, but there’s a solution you can trust. I’ve researched for years—Bitcoin offers growth and stability to weather the dollar’s ups and downs.

Why the Dollar Struggles

Central banks, like the Federal Reserve, print money to fund deficits or stimulate the economy—like after 2020. Since the dollar left the gold standard in 1971, there’s no limit. From 2020-2025, the money supply grew 38% (Federal Reserve data), driving up prices.

The Impact on Your Savings

Inflation means your $100 in 2020 buys only ~$82 today (CPI data). Spikes, like 9% in 2022, hit savers hard, while asset owners gain. It’s a slow, unpredictable loss—but you don’t have to face it alone.

Bitcoin: A Trusted Solution

After years of research, I believe Bitcoin is a steady anchor for most people. Its supply is fixed at 21 million coins, resisting inflation’s erosion. It’s grown ~4,000% from 2015-2025, with a market cap of ~$1.2 trillion (May 2025, CoinMarketCap).

Many fear Bitcoin’s volatility—prices dropped 20% in Q1 2025—or don’t understand it. But its fundamentals are solid: a decentralized network, running without downtime since 2009. It’s a choice for growth you can hold onto while the dollar sinks.

Other Options to Consider

Bitcoin isn’t the only path. Here are other categories, though they may not match Bitcoin’s unique strengths:

Crypto (Beyond Bitcoin): Includes Ethereum (~$350 billion market cap), which powers apps like DeFi. High growth potential, but volatile and prone to scams.

Stocks: S&P 500 ETFs average ~10% annual returns (Yahoo Finance). Good for growth, but market dips (like 20% in 2022) can hurt.

Bonds: Treasuries yield ~3-4% (2025, Treasury.gov). Low risk, but returns often lag inflation (e.g., 9% in 2022).

Property: Real estate appreciates ~5% annually (Zillow, 2020-2025). Stable, but high costs (median U.S. home ~$400,000) and illiquidity are challenges.

Take the First Step

Inflation is a challenge, but you can find comfort with Bitcoin—a choice I trust after years of research. Start with Bitcoin and grow steadily, even as the dollar fluctuates. It’s a solid foundation to build on.
