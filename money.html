Why Your Money Loses Value

Governments print money endlessly, driving up prices. Learn why this happens, the problems it causes, and how to save smarter without leaving the dollar system.

Why Money Gets Printed Endlessly

Central banks, like the Federal Reserve, control the money supply. They print more to fund government deficits, stimulate the economy, or handle crises—like after 2020. Since the dollar isn’t tied to gold (post-1971), there’s no limit. From 2020-2025, the money supply grew 38% (Federal Reserve data), pushing prices higher.

The Problems It Causes

Printing money causes inflation—prices for groceries, gas, and rent rise. Your $100 in 2020 buys only ~$82 today (CPI data). High spikes, like 9% in 2022, hurt savers and retirees most. It also widens inequality: cash loses value, but asset owners (stocks, real estate) gain.

Solutions to Protect Your Savings

You don’t need to ditch the dollar—just invest it wisely. Here are some options:

  • Stocks: ETFs like the S&P 500 average ~10% annual returns, but markets can dip.
  • Bonds: Treasury bonds yield ~3-4% (2025 data), offering safety with lower returns.
  • Real Estate: Property grows with inflation, but requires big capital.
  • Gold: A stable store of value over centuries, though less liquid.
  • Bitcoin: Fixed at 21 million coins, some use it as a hedge, but it’s volatile (20% drop Q1 2025) and has a bad rap for scams.

Next Steps

Inflation won’t stop, but you can act. Research stocks for growth, bonds for safety, or real estate for stability. Start small, learn fast, and pick what suits your needs—all while keeping your dollars.
