How Inflation Affects You

Let’s explore why prices keep rising and how you can protect your savings.

Why Prices Keep Rising

Governments print money to fund spending, like after 2020. From 2020 to 2025, the money supply grew 38% (Federal Reserve data), driving up prices.

What It Means for You

Your $100 in 2020 buys only ~$82 today (CPI data). Inflation spikes, like 9% in 2022, hit savers hardest.

Ways to Protect Your Savings

Here are four options to consider. Think about what fits your goals.

Crypto

Digital currencies like Bitcoin (capped at 21 million coins) and Ethereum. Bitcoin grew ~4,000% since 2015 but dropped 20% in Q1 2025.

Risks: Volatility, scams, regulations.

Consider: Are you okay with high risk for potential growth?

Stocks

Shares in companies or ETFs like the S&P 500, averaging ~10% annual returns. The S&P 500 fell 20% in 2022.

Risks: Market dips, company performance.

Consider: Do you want steady growth over time?

Bonds

Debt securities like Treasuries, yielding ~3-4%. They lagged inflation at 9% in 2022.

Risks: Lower returns, interest rate changes.

Consider: Do you value safety over growth?

Property

Real estate or REITs, growing ~5% annually. U.S. homes average ~$400,000.

Risks: High costs, maintenance, illiquidity.

Consider: Can you invest in a tangible asset?

Reflect and Decide

Inflation is a challenge, but you have options. Which path feels right for you?
