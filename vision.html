The Vision of Satoshi

In 2008, an unknown person or group using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto introduced Bitcoin to the world. Their vision of decentralized, sound money continues to reshape our modern economy.

2009-01-03

"The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks"

The Bitcoin genesis block contained this headline, forever timestamping the motivation behind Bitcoin's creation.

2009-02-11

"The root problem with conventional currency is all the trust that's required to make it work..."

Satoshi explains why the world needs a trustless form of money.

2010-07-29

"If you don't believe me or don't get it, I don't have time to try to convince you, sorry."

Satoshi's response when questioned about Bitcoin's security model.

October 31, 2008

Satoshi Nakamoto publishes the Bitcoin whitepaper, introducing a revolutionary peer-to-peer electronic cash system.

January 3, 2009

The Bitcoin network goes live with the genesis block, containing a powerful message about the banking system.

January 12, 2009

The first Bitcoin transaction occurs between Satoshi and Hal Finney, marking the beginning of the Bitcoin economy.

December 12, 2010

Satoshi's last known public post, leaving Bitcoin in the hands of the community while remaining anonymous.

"The nature of Bitcoin is such that once version 0.1 was released, the core design was set in stone for the rest of its lifetime."

Satoshi Nakamoto
